Core Topics

📐 Kinematics

▼

Kinematics describes the motion of objects without referring to the forces that cause motion.

Key Concepts

  • Displacement (Δx) — change in position (vector)
  • Velocity (v) — rate of change of displacement; avg = Δx / Δt
  • Acceleration (a) — rate of change of velocity; avg = Δv / Δt

Kinematic Equations (constant acceleration)

v = v₀ + at
x = x₀ + v₀t + ½at²
v² = v₀² + 2aΔx
x = x₀ + ½(v₀ + v)t

💡 Tip: In free fall, use a = g = 9.8 m/s² (downward).

🔭 Newton's Laws of Motion

▼

Newton's three laws form the foundation of classical mechanics.

1st Law — Law of Inertia

An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion at constant velocity, unless acted upon by a net external force.

2nd Law — F = ma

The net force on an object equals its mass times its acceleration.

ΣF = ma

3rd Law — Action & Reaction

For every action force, there is an equal and opposite reaction force.

F₁₂ = −F₂₁

Common Forces

  • Weight: W = mg
  • Normal Force (N): perpendicular to the surface
  • Friction: f = μN (kinetic: μₖ, static: μₛ ≥ μₖ)
  • Tension (T): force transmitted through a rope/string

💡 Tip: Always draw a Free-Body Diagram (FBD) before writing equations.

⚡ Work, Energy & Power

▼

Work

W = F · d · cos θ

Work is done only when a force has a component parallel to displacement.

Kinetic Energy

KE = ½mv²

Gravitational Potential Energy

PE = mgh

Work–Energy Theorem

W_net = ΔKE

Conservation of Mechanical Energy

KE₁ + PE₁ = KE₂ + PE₂ (no friction)

Power

P = W / t = Fv

💡 Tip: If friction is present, include the work done by friction (negative) on the energy side.

🎱 Momentum & Impulse

▼

Momentum

p = mv

Impulse–Momentum Theorem

J = FΔt = Δp

Conservation of Momentum

In the absence of external forces, the total momentum of a system is conserved.

p₁ + p₂ = p₁' + p₂'

Types of Collisions

  • Elastic: both momentum and KE are conserved
  • Inelastic: momentum conserved; KE lost
  • Perfectly Inelastic: objects stick together after collision

💡 Tip: In perfectly inelastic collisions use: m₁v₁ + m₂v₂ = (m₁ + m₂)v'

🌍 Circular Motion & Gravitation

▼

Uniform Circular Motion

ac = v² / r
Fc = mac = mv² / r

Period & Frequency

T = 2πr / v
f = 1 / T

Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation

F = G · m₁m₂ / r²

G = 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg²

💡 Tip: Centripetal force is always directed toward the center of the circle.

〰️ Waves & Sound

▼

Wave Properties

  • Wavelength (λ) — distance between two identical points on a wave
  • Frequency (f) — number of cycles per second (Hz)
  • Amplitude (A) — maximum displacement from equilibrium

Wave Speed

v = fλ

Period

T = 1 / f

Transverse vs. Longitudinal

  • Transverse: oscillation ⊥ propagation (light, water waves)
  • Longitudinal: oscillation ∥ propagation (sound waves)

Speed of sound in air ≈ 343 m/s at 20 °C

💡 Tip: Higher frequency = higher pitch; larger amplitude = louder sound.
